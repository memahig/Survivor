# PEG Doctrine

**Persuasion–Evidence Gap**

**Status:** Structural Analysis Doctrine (v1.1)
**Subordinate to:** BiasLens Constitution

---

## Core Principle

The Persuasion–Evidence Gap (PEG) exists when the persuasive force of an argument exceeds the evidentiary support presented for its claims.

Persuasive signals increase reader confidence.
Evidence determines whether that confidence is justified.

When persuasive signals significantly exceed evidentiary grounding, the argument may move the reader further than the evidence alone can justify.

PEG identifies this structural condition.

PEG does not determine truth.
It evaluates information recoverability.

---

## Persuasion Signals

Persuasion signals amplify reader confidence independent of evidence.

BiasLens recognizes multiple tiers.

---

### Surface Signals

Common rhetorical amplifiers.

Examples:

- certainty language
- authority cues
- existential framing
- sweeping generalizations

Surface signals are common rhetorical devices and are not inherently problematic.
They become significant when combined with weak evidentiary grounding.

---

### Structural Signals

Persuasive mechanisms embedded in argument structure.

Examples:

- omission-dependent reasoning
- unsupported causal narratives
- scope inflation
- load-bearing weak claims

---

### Elite Persuasion Signals

Sophisticated techniques common in high-authority discourse.

Examples:

- performative concession
- definitional loading
- thesis insurance
- narrative compression

---

## Evidence Grounding

Evidence strength reflects how strongly claims are supported by verifiable information and disciplined reasoning.

---

### Surface Evidence

Basic evidence indicators:

- citations
- references
- source attribution

Presence alone does not guarantee strength.

---

### Structural Evidence

Indicators of disciplined reasoning:

- engagement with counterarguments
- demonstrated causal chains
- proportional claim scope
- comparative analysis
- comparative baselines for superlative claims

---

### Deep Evidence

Signals of high epistemic rigor:

- steel-manning opposing arguments
- explicit acknowledgement of limitations
- proportionate hedging of conclusions
- consistent analytical frameworks

---

## Load-Bearing Priority (The Tilt)

PEG is not an average measure.

BiasLens evaluates persuasion–evidence mismatch with special attention to an argument's load-bearing center.

Evidence supporting peripheral claims does not offset a persuasion–evidence gap in the claims carrying the argument's central conclusion.

An argument may therefore contain substantial factual content while still exhibiting a severe PEG at its structural core.

---

## Fragility Sensitivity Principle

PEG evaluation is sensitive to argument fragility.

When an argument's structural integrity is already weak — for example when key claims lack evidence or load-bearing claims are unsupported — the epistemic significance of omitted perspectives increases.

In such cases, missing counterarguments or contextual information carry greater diagnostic weight.

An absent perspective matters more when the argument would collapse if that perspective were considered.

This principle reflects a core BiasLens assumption:

Omissions are most consequential when they protect a structurally fragile conclusion.

Accordingly, BiasLens may elevate the significance of omissions when:

- argument fragility is high
- load-bearing claims show weak support
- the missing context would materially challenge the central conclusion

This adjustment does not presume author intent.

It reflects the structural reality that weak arguments are more dependent on excluded context to remain persuasive.

---

## Mechanism-Based Reporting

BiasLens does not report PEG as a numerical score.

Instead it identifies the specific mechanisms that create the gap.

These may include:

- omitted rival explanations
- unsupported causal claims
- framing escalation
- definitional loading
- narrative compression
- structurally weak load-bearing claims

Readers see how the argument works, not a rating.

---

## Reader Warning Principle

When PEG becomes significant, BiasLens warns the reader that persuasive structure may exceed evidentiary grounding.

Warnings describe observable reasoning patterns.

They do not speculate about author motives.

---

## Self-Application Clause

PEG analysis applies to all texts without exception, including:

- journalism
- scholarly work
- institutional reports
- political commentary
- BiasLens outputs

No analytical authority is exempt from structural scrutiny.

---

## Rendering Philosophy

PEG rendering follows these principles:

- Describe mechanisms, not the object.
- Do not label a text as propaganda.
- Use severity-based wording to describe elements commonly used in propaganda-style persuasion.
- Keep PEG separate from future Epistemic Success signals.
- PEG is a structural gap detector, not a truth detector.

---

## Canonical Summary

The Persuasion–Evidence Gap exists when:

The confidence a text asks from the reader exceeds the confidence its evidence can justify.
